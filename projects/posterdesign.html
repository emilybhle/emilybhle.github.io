Poster Design

Visual Development and Illustration

The posters above are from the Summer Themed Entertainment Project (STEP). As the Marketing Lead on that project, I created various attractions posters for the conceptual theme park, Stargrove. These posters are heavily influenced by Disneyland’s original attractions posters from in the 50’s - 60’s.

The left poster was created for the downtown area’s roller coaster. The coaster was such a great concept of an alien version of a DMV where guests would board a test ride with an instructor. I knew I wanted to incorporate their flying vehicle within the imagery.

The poster to the right was created for a flat ride themed around the history of space travel. The attraction's ride vehicle was influenced by rotors and within the attraction guests would be "abducted" from a UFO.

These are posters I created for my Wednesday night Dungeons and Dragons group. At the time we were running a campaign using Stras Acimovic’s “Yesterday’s Tomorrow”. It was a great campaign themed around the world of cyberpunk. As we were meeting every week, everyone was creating such great characters that it inspired me to make a movie poster for our group. In the final version on the left, I opted to use bright colours in a single-line style to mimic a neon motif based on the 80’s influence of the cyperpunk aesthetic. I was also heavily influenced by classic Marvel Cinematic Universe posters like the ones for the Avengers. In the middle of each poster, you can see the largest form, our trusted Dungeon Master.
